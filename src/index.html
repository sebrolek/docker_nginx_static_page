Apes

Chimpanzee

Known for their high intelligence and close genetic relation to humans, chimpanzees inhabit the forests and savannas of tropical Africa. They are social animals, living in communities led by an alpha male.

Gorilla

Gorillas are the largest primates and are native to Africa. They are divided into two species: the eastern gorilla and the western gorilla. Gorillas are known for their strength and gentle demeanor.

Orangutan

Found in the rainforests of Borneo and Sumatra, orangutans are known for their distinctive red hair and arboreal lifestyle. They are highly intelligent and are one of the closest relatives to humans.

Bonobo

Also known as the pygmy chimpanzee, bonobos are closely related to chimpanzees. They are found in the dense forests of the Democratic Republic of Congo. Bonobos are known for their peaceful and egalitarian social structure.

Spider Monkey

Spider monkeys are native to Central and South America. They are known for their long, slender limbs and prehensile tails, which they use to navigate through the forest canopy.
